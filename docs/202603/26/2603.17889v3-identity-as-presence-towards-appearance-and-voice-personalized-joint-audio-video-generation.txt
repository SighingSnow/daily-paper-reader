Abstract. Recent advances have demonstrated compelling capabilities in synthesizing real individuals into generated videos, reflecting the grow-ing demand for identity-aware content creation. Nevertheless, an openly accessible framework enabling fine-grained control over facial appear-ance and voice timbre across multiple identities remains unavailable. In this work, we present a unified and scalable framework for identity-aware joint audio-video generation, enabling high-fidelity and consistent personalization. Specifically, we introduce a data curation pipeline that automatically extracts identity-bearing information with paired anno-tations across audio and visual modalities, covering diverse scenarios from single-subject to multi-subject interactions. We further propose a flexible and scalable identity injection mechanism for single- and multi-subject scenarios, in which both facial appearance and vocal timbre act as identity-bearing control signals. Moreover, in light of modality dispar-ity, we design a multi-stage training strategy to accelerate convergence and enforce cross-modal coherence. Experiments demonstrate the superi-ority of the proposed framework. For more details and qualitative results, please refer to our webpage: Identity-as-Presence. 

Keywords: joint audio-video generation · personalized video generation 

## 1 Introduction 

Recent advances in audio-visual generation have established a strong founda-tion for high-fidelity multimodal synthesis. Building on this progress, emerging applications have created increasing demand for personalized video generation that faithfully replicates both the facial appearance and vocal timbre of real individuals. This trend highlights the need for models with cameo capabilities ,enabling a specific person to speak arbitrary content and appear in diverse scenes conditioned on user input. Such functionality is critical for applications includ-ing virtual avatars and digital identity preservation, which require precise and disentangled control over facial identity and voice characteristics.

A straightforward solution is a multi-stage cascade. It first generates audio and video independently, and then performs audio-driven lip synchronization. However, such sequential pipelines impose rigid visual constraints and offer lim-ited support for cinematic dynamics. They also model audio mainly through lip motion rather than holistic sound, which weakens overall audio-visual coherence. These limitations make end-to-end generation a more promising paradigm for unified audio-visual synthesis. Despite this potential, building a unified cameo model remains challenging. Identity-controllable audio-video generation requires stable cross-modal iden-tity consistency and flexible voice-face binding in multi-identity scenarios. In addition, high-quality identity-labeled audio-visual datasets are scarce, making stable and scalable training difficult. Existing identity injection methods remain largely unimodal. In audio generation, most approaches rely on autoregressive (AR) voice cloning frameworks, which excel at reproducing the voice timbre of a target individual from a reference utterance [33, 34, 39]. While diffusion-based models have recently emerged in speech and music synthesis with limited control over individual identity [13, 20]. In video generation, facial identity is typically preserved by encoding reference images through cross-attention [15,47,48], sym-metric network branches [16, 50], or token-wise concatenation [12, 29, 46]. These methods enable faithful visual identity retention but operate solely in the vi-sual domain. While recent works have made strides in multi-identity spatial composition via region-based attention masks [10, 41] or anchored prompts [27], extending this capability to joint audio-video generation remains an open chal-lenge. Most critically, the cross-modal binding of multiple individuals is largely unexplored. Unlike spatial composition in static images, joint audio-visual gen-eration requires the model to dynamically associate specific voice timbres with their corresponding facial appearances over time. Current systems lack a unified mechanism for such cross-modal identity alignment, resulting in inconsistent or ambiguous identity mapping, especially in multi-subject scenarios. As a result, Identity as Presence 3

the problem of coherent, fine-grained control over multiple audio-visual identities remains fundamentally unsolved. To this end, we present Identity-as-Presence , a unified and scalable frame-work for appearance- and voice-personalized joint audio-video generation. To address the critical data bottleneck, we introduce a fully automated data cu-ration pipeline that constructs high-quality, identity-labeled audio-visual pairs without manual annotation, ensuring precise face-voice alignment for complex multi-identity interactions. To enable multimodal identity injection, we propose a unified identity injection mechanism. Specifically, visual (one-shot or multi-view facial images) and auditory identity control signals are encoded and aligned through shared identity embeddings and structured spatiotemporal positional embeddings. These embeddings ensure consistent face-voice association for mul-tiple individuals in complex scenarios while effectively distinguishing identities and preserving structural coherence. The resulting representations are then pro-cessed by a dual-tower diffusion transformer, where a decoupled parameterization with asymmetric self-attention is introduced to stabilize optimization. Further-more, to leverage heterogeneous data across modalities and promote progressive audio-visual alignment, we design a multi-stage training strategy. The model first learns unimodal identity injection on large-scale unimodal data, then jointly op-timizes multimodal identity injection, and finally performs fine-tuning for multi-view identity modeling. Together, these designs enable high-fidelity and coherent synthesis of personalized multi-subject audio-visual content with precise identity control. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of audio quality, video fidelity, and cross-modal alignment. Our main contributions are threefold. 

– We introduce a fully automated data curation pipeline that constructs high-quality, identity-labeled audio-visual pairs without manual annotation. It effectively alleviates the critical scarcity of paired multimodal data, providing a robust foundation for learning complex multi-identity interactions. 

– We propose a unified identity injection mechanism comprising multimodal information representation, structured spatiotemporal alignment, and decou-pled attention parameterization. Together, these components resolve identity entanglement while ensuring strict cross-modal alignment. 

– We devise a multi-stage training strategy to mitigate optimization instability arising from cross-modal discrepancies. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance in audio generation, visual synthesis, and cross-modal alignment. Our dataset and model will be publicly released to promote transparency, reproducibility, and further research in the AIGC community. 

## 2 Related Work 

Identity-Aware Video Generation. Prior work on identity-aware video gen-eration either injects facial identity via explicit encoders or regularizes identity 4 Y. Chen et al. 

consistency within diffusion backbones. ID-animator [15] conditions a text-to-video diffusion model with a lightweight face adapter and learnable facial latent queries, while ConsistID [48] enforces temporal identity coherence through fre-quency decomposition in diffusion transformers. Subject-consistent frameworks, such as Phantom [29], SkyReels-A2 [12] and VACE [21], concatenate reference conditions with noisy latents and perform full-sequence denoising to stabilize identity over time. With video foundation models, multimodal customization methods such as HunyuanCustom [17] and ConceptMaster [18] integrate con-ditions via modality-specific modules, but often incur substantial training cost. Stand-In [46] introduces a lightweight, plug-and-play identity control design to reduce trainable parameters. However, these approaches focus exclusively on vi-sual fidelity, thereby overlooking the holistic nature of identity that binds facial features with specific vocal characteristics. In contrast, our approach achieves multimodal identity-aware joint audio-video generation, ensuring cross-modal identity consistency. 

Identity-Aware Audio Generation. Recent advancements in text-to-speech (TTS) have achieved human-level naturalness and high-fidelity speaker cloning [24, 25, 33, 34, 37], generally falling into two paradigms: Autoregressive (AR) and Non-Autoregressive (NAR). AR models, exemplified by VALL-E [39], treat TTS as a language modeling task, achieving remarkable speaker similarity but of-ten suffering from slow inference speeds due to sequential processing. In con-trast, NAR models offer superior efficiency through parallel processing. Promi-nent flow matching models like Voicebox [22] and Matcha-TTS [32] condition flow trajectories on text and audio, while E2 TTS [11] simplified the pipeline by discarding phoneme alignments in favor of direct subjec-level modeling. F5-TTS [6] further refined this architecture by introducing Sway Sampling to rectify alignment flaws and achieve robust, high-fidelity zero-shot generation. However, these methods typically rely on audio infilling for voice cloning, a paradigm ill-suited for the arbitrary text generation required in our Cameo scenario. While joint audio-video models favor diffusion backbones, adapting them for robust voice injection remains challenging. To address this, we introduce a streamlined token-concatenation mechanism for voice identity injection. Remarkably, with minimal architectural modifications, our approach achieves voice cloning perfor-mance comparable to state-of-the-art NAR TTS models. 

Joint Audio-Visual Generation. To bridge the gap between visual and au-ditory generation, recent research has pivoted towards joint audio-video gen-eration. Consequently, unified frameworks have emerged as a promising direc-tion to model the intricate dependencies between sight and sound. Pioneer-ing works [14, 28, 31, 40, 49] attempt to synthesize both modalities simultane-ously within a single model. These methods predominantly adopt a dual-tower paradigm. Ovi [31] and LTX-2 [14] employ a symmetric architecture, while Jav-isDit [28], Universe-1 [40], and UniAVGen [49] employ asymmetric designs aug-mented with intricate interaction modules to facilitate cross-modal fusion. How-ever, despite these architectural innovations, existing unified methods often lack precise control over specific identities and struggle with identity entanglement Identity as Presence 5

in multi-subject scenarios, leading to erroneous face-voice bindings such as voice swapping. In contrast, we propose a unified framework with a multimodal iden-tity injection approach, enabling coherent, multi-identity generation where flex-ible visual inputs are strictly aligned with their corresponding vocal timbres. 

## 3 Identity-as-Presence 

We propose Identity-as-Presence, a unified framework for joint audio-video gen-eration that achieves coherent multi-identity control, consisting of three key as-pects: an automated data curation pipeline for extracting identity-labeled audio-visual clips, a unified identity injection approach that independently modulates visual and auditory identity features, and a multi-stage training strategy de-signed to address modality disparity and ensure stable convergence.

3.2 Data Curation Pipeline 

High-quality, identity-consistent data is the cornerstone of personalized audio-visual generation. To address the scarcity of paired multimodal data, we develop a fully automated data curation pipeline. As illustrated in Figure 2, the pipeline operates in three cascaded phases: (1) Multimodal Identity Extraction, which extracts fine-grained identity-bearing information from raw videos; (2) Multi-modal Captioning, which leverages multimodal large language models (MLLMs) to synthesize comprehensive textual descriptions; and (3) Audio-Visual Identity Matching, which associates identities across clips for accurate identity injection. 6 Y. The process involves isolating both visual and audi-tory identity-specific signals from raw videos, synthesizing comprehensive captions via MLLMs, and rigorously matching audio-visual identities to guarantee precise alignment across video clips to ensure high-fidelity identity consistency.

1. Multimodal Identity Extraction. For the visual stream, we first ap-ply multi-object tracking to isolate individual subjects within raw videos. Frame-wise human detection is performed using YOLOv11 [23], followed by long-term tracking with MOTRv2 [51]. For each tracked subject, we per-form face detection and estimate FLAME [26] parameters using SMIRK [35], capturing dense 3D facial geometry and expression details. The resulting 3D parameters are then used to extract high-quality one-shot or diverse multi-view reference images, which serve as robust facial identity control signals for our generative model. Handling the audio modality requires separating mixed vocal tracks. We use Demucs [8] to separate vocal and background components and apply 3D-Speaker [5] for speaker diarization, isolating the distinct voice of each speaker. Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) is used to transcribe the spoken content. To align facial appearance with vocal timbre, we employ SyncNet [7] to compute the synchronization confidence between the sepa-rated audio and visual lip movements. Only pairs with high synchronization scores are retained, ensuring that the extracted voice timbre is correctly associated with the corresponding facial identity. 2. Multimodal Captioning. Finally, we use MLLMs to generate textual de-scriptions for each video clip. Specifically, we feed each triplet consisting of the video clip, the extracted facial images for each subject, and the ASR transcripts into Qwen3-Omni [45]. Using a carefully designed prompt tem-plate, we instruct the model to generate comprehensive descriptions covering camera movements, scene context, ambience and sound effects, and subject descriptions, including subject appearance, actions, expressions, and speech content. Moreover, to establish robust subject anchors, we explicitly require Identity as Presence 7

the model to bind physical appearance attributes and spoken content to unique identifiers for each subject. As shown in the right part of Figure 2, these structured captions serve as the text prompts for our joint audio-video generation model. 3. Audio-Visual Identity Matching. To ensure the model learns to inject identity rather than simply copy and paste, we construct training pairs via an extra matching phase. Specifically, the reference facial images are supposed to be diverse in identity-irrelevant attributes such as head pose and expres-sion, and the reference audio must not share causal transcription links with the target video. To achieve this, we implement a strict cross-clip matching strategy. First, we group video clips by visual identity using face embeddings extracted by ArcFace [9]. However, visual consistency does not guarantee vo-cal consistency due to dubbing or voice-overs. Therefore, we further refine these groups using speaker embeddings extracted by ERes2Net [4]. We com-pute the cosine similarity between speaker embeddings to filter out pairs with low similarity, ensuring that our training pairs share the same appearance and voice identity while containing diverse, non-overlapping speech content. Our data curation pipeline ensures precise alignment between facial appear-ance and vocal timbre, through which we construct a large-scale identity-labeled audio-visual dataset encompassing diverse scenarios ranging from single-subject to multi-subject interactions. The resulting dataset is denoted as D = {(v, a, c v ,ca, txt )}, where v and a represent the video and audio modalities, and cv and 

ca correspond to facial appearance and vocal timbre. D serves as the founda-tion for our model, providing robust supervision to learn the disentangled yet synchronized identity signals required by our Identity-as-Presence framework.

A critical challenge in this paradigm is the effective injection of identity control signals, specifically facial appearance and vocal timbre, into the generative process in a manner that is both disentangled and coherent. Structured Position Embeddings. Proper positional injection is crucial for the model to distinguish between identities and maintain structural coherence. For the video and audio latent tokens zvt and zat , we apply standard factorized 3D RoPE [36] and scale the audio temporal channels to align with the video frame rate. For the identity tokens ˆrv and ˆra, positional assignment requires a specialized strategy to ensure numerical stability and cross-modal binding. To this end, we propose a virtual temporal extension mechanism that assigns deterministic coordinates to identity tokens beyond the generation timeline. Recognizing that facial appearance identity ˆrv is inherently encoded in the spatial domain while vocal timbre identity ˆra unfolds along the temporal dimen-sion, we establish a strict geometric alignment to bridge these heterogeneous modalities. We map the spatially organized visual tokens ˆrv to virtual temporal indices, assigning Tv, max to visual tokens of the first identity, Tv, max +1 to the sec-ond, and continuing sequentially. Correspondingly, the auditory identity tokens 

ˆra are arranged along the temporal axis starting from Ta, max . We synchronize this placement such that the k-th auditory identity (at Ta, max +( k−1) ×L, where 

L denotes the maximum length of the audio reference) aligns strictly with the 

k-th visual identity. This creates an invariant geometric anchor , allowing the attention mechanism to utilize relative positional distances as a strong inductive bias for binding spatial facial appearance with the corresponding temporal vocal timbre. We further propose a strategy for multi-view facial tokens. Instead of linearly concatenating reference images, which often leads to out-of-distribution spatial coordinates and frequency extrapolation artifacts, we employ a dense near-square packing strategy for multi-view facial images. We organize the multi-view images into a compact grid layout within (H, W ) before patchification. The resulting visual tokens ˆrv are then assigned a specific virtual temporal position. This layout constrains the maximum spatial frequencies to a stable range seen during training, significantly improving the robustness of the rotary embedding against spatial distortions.

3.4 Training Strategy 

To leverage heterogeneous data across modalities and facilitate progressive audio-visual alignment, we adopt a multi-stage training strategy motivated by dispar-ities in data availability. Identity-labeled audio data (e.g., TTS corpora) is the most abundant, identity-labeled video data is more limited due to filtering re-quirements, and paired identity-labeled audio-visual data is the scarcest because of strict synchronization and semantic constraints. Directly training on limited paired data risks overfitting and poor identity diversity. We therefore propose a progressive learning framework with three stages: unimodal identity training, joint multimodal identity training, and multi-view identity fine-tuning.

Stage 1: Unimodal Identity Training. We first decouple train-ing to fully exploit large-scale uni-modal data and learn identity-specific priors before cross-modal alignment. The audio tower is trained on large TTS datasets to model diverse vocal timbres and prosody, enabling robust voice identity injection. Notably, we find that the uni-modal pre-training stage is the optimal window for instilling robust multilingual capabilities. This stage imposes lower data requirements while ensuring that the acquired capabilities are well-preserved during post-training. We conduct exper-iments on TTS benchmarks to demonstrate that after the unimodal training stage, our audio tower achieves comparable performance to non-autoregressive (NAR) TTS models, as shown in Table 1. In parallel, the video tower is trained on identity-labeled video datasets to learn appearance and motion dynamics. During this stage, cross-modal fusion layers are masked, allowing each modality to converge optimally on its respective data. 

Stage 2: Joint Multimodal Identity Training. Then, we perform joint mul-timodal training using curated paired identity-labeled audio-visual data. The model is initialized from Stage 1 and cross-modal fusion layers are activated to Identity as Presence 11 

learn temporal alignment between speech and facial motion. This decoupling al-lows large unimodal datasets to provide identity diversity while a smaller paired set enables precise audio-visual synchronization. 

Stage 3: Multi-view Identity Fine-tuning. To overcome the limitations of 2D one-shot generation, we introduce a lightweight fine-tuning stage using a small set of high-quality multi-view identity data. This stage improves spatial awareness and injects holistic identity consistency, enabling avatars to maintain structural integrity and photorealism under large pose variations.

## 5 Conclusion 

In this work, we presented Identity-as-Presence, a pioneering framework that addresses the critical challenges of personalized joint audio-video generation. By moving beyond the limitations of rigid cascaded pipelines and unimodal ap-proaches, we established a robust end-to-end framework capable of simultaneous, high-fidelity injection of both facial appearance and vocal timbre. Our approach effectively resolves the complexities of cross-modal consistency and multi-identity attribute binding through three synergistic innovations, i.e ., an automated data curation pipeline that alleviates the scarcity of identity-labeled audio-visual paired data, a unified identity injection mechanism that ensures precise face-voice association even in complex multi-subject scenarios, and a multi-stage training strategy that harmonizes the optimization of heterogeneous modalities.
